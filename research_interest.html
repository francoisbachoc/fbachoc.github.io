Gaussian process modeling

My main research interests are related to Gaussian process modeling. These models are also called Kriging models with Gaussian processes. The Gaussian processes website provides books and tutorials on this field.

Covariance function estimation

I work on the issue of the covariance function estimation for Gaussian processes. In this context, I study the Maximum Likelihood and Cross Validation estimators. I also study the influence of the irregularity of the sampling on the estimation, in an increasing-domain asymptotic context. Finally, more recently, I worked on increasing-domain asymptotic results for multivariate tapering.

On the subject of the covariance function estimation, I work as a member of the MASCOT NUM research group and the ReDICE consortium.

Uncertainty quantification for computer experiments

I am also particularly interested in the application of Gaussian process models to computer experiments, either in a context where field data are considered (computer model validation), or not (surrogate modeling of computer models).

On the subject of computer experiments, I participated in the Sub-Project 4: Model Validation & Calibration, Sensitivity and Uncertainty Quantification of the European NURISP project. I also work in relation with the MANON research laboratory.

Small-probability estimation

I participated in the CEMRACS 2013 summer school. There I worked, together with two other PhD students, on a small-probability estimation problem arising from a shielding study in neutron transport. We adapted a general interacting-particle method to the specificities of the neutron-transport problem.

Inference post-model-selection

Since I started my post-doctoral position at the Department of Statistics and Operations Research in Vienna, I have been interested in the area of inference post-model-selection in linear regression. More specifically, I worked on uniformly valid confidence intervals post-model-selection.
